  • Database Automation Policies

    • Database Automation Policies

    At BBS, we prioritize the scalability, flexibility, and extendability of all database solutions. In order to ensure optimal performance and maintainability, we implement automation techniques such as stored procedures and triggers to streamline data processes. However, to preserve flexibility and allow for easy customization and future extension, we adhere to the following guidelines:

    1. Limited Use of Stored Procedures and Triggers

    For each database table, a maximum of two stored procedures and/or triggers will be implemented. These automation tools will only be used when absolutely necessary to achieve specific business requirements or optimize performance. This limitation ensures that the database remains easily customizable, scalable, and maintainable, allowing for future expansions and modifications without unnecessary complexity.

    2. Need-Based Implementation

    Stored procedures and triggers will be created only where their use is justified based on the complexity and requirements of the database system. We aim to minimize their implementation in order to avoid performance degradation and to preserve the ability for quick, custom updates. Every use case for automation will be carefully evaluated to ensure it provides clear and significant benefits to the overall system's efficiency.

    3. Customization and Extensibility

    Our approach is designed to balance automation with customization. While we integrate stored procedures and triggers where needed, we also ensure that clients can easily modify and extend their systems without being hindered by excessive automation. This allows clients to adapt the database as their business grows and new requirements arise.

    4. Documentation and Support

    Each stored procedure and trigger will be fully documented, ensuring that clients can understand their function and make any necessary adjustments in the future. Our support team is also available to assist clients with any questions or changes related to these automated processes, ensuring that they remain empowered to manage their databases effectively.

  • Encryption Policy

    • Encryption Policy

    Clients have the option to request MySQL database encryption during the order process. We utilize the AES_Encrypt command for this purpose. It is essential to understand that while encryption significantly enhances data security, it is advisable to use it judiciously and primarily in scenarios where sensitive information needs protection.

    For databases such as PostgreSQL, we support encryption through industry-standard methods, ensuring your data remains secure. PostgreSQL offers robust encryption features, which can be applied with ease. For SQLite, while encryption is not natively supported, we recommend utilizing third-party extensions, such as SQLCipher, for implementing encryption in your SQLite databases.

    Microsoft Access, however, does not natively support database-level encryption. While it is possible to protect individual objects within the database (like setting passwords on databases or encrypting data within fields), it does not provide a comprehensive encryption solution for the entire database system. Clients should be aware of this limitation when choosing Microsoft Access for their database needs.

    Encryption may result in a performance loss during data analysis, so we encourage clients to carefully evaluate whether the benefits of encrypting specific data outweigh the potential impact on system performance. Additionally, clients must allocate a secure location for storing the passwords associated with the encrypted data. This is crucial for maintaining both the integrity and accessibility of the encryption process.

    We will provide the passwords for the encrypted columns; however, the responsibility for securely storing these passwords lies entirely with the client. By opting for encryption, clients acknowledge and accept these terms and conditions, ensuring they are fully aware of the implications and responsibilities associated with encrypting their databases.
